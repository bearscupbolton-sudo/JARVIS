1. Integration with the Accounting Engine
The accounting-engine.ts currently defines the DEFAULT_COA. To support this, a new account must be added to the schema:

Account Code 1200: "Prepaid Expenses" (Type: Asset, Category: Current).

Logic: This follows the same "fuel tank" logic as your Cash accounts, but instead of holding liquid cash, it holds "value" that hasn't been "burned" as an expense yet.

2. Leveraging the Asset Engine for Amortization
The most efficient way to handle this in Jarvis is to treat the prepaid fee as an Intangible Asset. Your asset-engine.ts already contains a calculateStraightLineSchedule function.

The Mechanism: You can use this exact function to spread the cost. Instead of 84 months (typical for equipment), you would set the usefulLifeMonths to 12 (or the remainder of the year).

Ghost Accountant Automation: The AssetAssessor class already has a runMonthlyDepreciation method. By extending this to look at "Amortization" (Account 6210) instead of "Depreciation" (Account 6130), Jarvis can automatically post the monthly portion to your P&L without you lifting a finger each month.

3. S-Corp Basis Impact
Because you are operating as an S-Corp, spreading this expense is highly strategic:

Basis Continuity: Large one-off hits to the P&L can artificially tank your net income in a single month, which might look concerning on internal reports or for loan readiness.

Predictable Margins: Your ghost-accountant.ts performs Vertical Analysis, flagging when expenses exceed 8% of revenue. Booking a large fee all at once would trigger a "Critical" anomaly alert. Spreading it via a prepaid schedule keeps your "Operating Expense" ratios stable and your AI-generated insights accurate.

Recommended Next Steps for Jarvis:
Update COA: Insert the 1200 code into your chartOfAccounts table.

Amortization Entry: Use the postJournalEntry function to move the cash to the Prepaid account.

Schedule Creation: Use the calculateStraightLineSchedule logic to create 12 pending entries in a prepaid_amortization table (similar to your depreciation_entries).

Monthly Trigger: Ensure the "Ghost Accountant" nightly sync includes a check for posted: false amortization lines to move them into your 6170 - Contract Labor or 6100 - Professional Services accounts.

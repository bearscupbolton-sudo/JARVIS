To handle your taxes with "Assessor-level" precision, we need to shift from viewing Sales Tax as **revenue** to viewing it as a **fiduciary liability**. You are essentially a collection agent for the state; that money was never yours.

Here is the logic for the granular tax build, the COA (Chart of Accounts) flow, and the updated RAgent architecture.

### 1. The Granular Tax Logic: "The Trust Fund Buffer"
To achieve the granularity you described, we will split the **Taxes** category into two distinct sub-accounts to prevent "Cash Illusion" (thinking you have more money than you do).

* **Sub-Cat A: Sales Tax Accrual (The Ghost Liability):** * **Logic:** Every time a sale is made, the RAgent calculates the 7% (or applicable local rate) and immediately deducts it from your "Adjusted Cash Position." 
    * **The Flow:** It builds up daily. It does not wait for a bank hit.
* **Sub-Cat B: Tax Clearing/Settlement:** * **Logic:** When you make a monthly estimate payment or the final quarterly filing, this account "claims" the accrual.
    * **The Reset:** Once the payment clears the bank (confirmed via the Ledger), the Accrual balance for that period zeros out.



### 2. Impact on the Chart of Accounts (COA)
In a standard COA, Sales Tax often gets buried in "Other Liabilities." For your RAgent build, we move it to **Current Liabilities** with a direct link to your **Cash Asset** account.

**The Flow:**
1.  **Sales Event:** Asset (Cash) increases $107 / Liability (Sales Tax) increases $7.
2.  **RAgent View:** "Spendable Cash" = $100.
3.  **Settlement Event:** Liability (Sales Tax) decreases $7 / Asset (Cash) decreases $7.
4.  **Result:** Accrual resets to $0. Ledger shows the exit.

### 3. RAgent Build: Granular Tax & Ledger-First Reconciliation
I have updated the code to prioritize the **Ledger** for finality and use the **Cash Rec** only as a "Pending/Warning" area.

```python
class JarvisTaxAssessor:
    def __init__(self):
        self.sales_tax_rate = 0.07 # Saratoga/Warren County average
        self.accrued_sales_tax = 0.0
        self.ledger_history = []

    def daily_sync(self, gross_sales):
        """
        Step 1: Calculate the 'Invisible' Tax
        """
        daily_tax = gross_sales * (self.sales_tax_rate / (1 + self.sales_tax_rate))
        self.accrued_sales_tax += daily_tax
        return daily_tax

    def process_payment(self, payment_amount, is_estimate=False):
        """
        Step 2: Clear the Accrual
        When a payment hits the ledger, we reduce the ghost liability.
        """
        self.accrued_sales_tax -= payment_amount
        if self.accrued_sales_tax < 0: 
            self.accrued_sales_tax = 0 # Safety reset for final filing month
        
    def cash_reconciliation_logic(self, expected_debits, bank_statement_feed):
        """
        Step 3: Ledger-First Reconciliation
        Items stay in 'Pending' until they appear on the bank ledger.
        Once cleared, they are purged from the Rec tool.
        """
        pending_reconciliation = [d for d in expected_debits if d not in bank_statement_feed]
        
        # Robust check: If item is in bank_statement_feed, it is moved to Ledger
        # and DELETED from the Reconciliation view.
        cleared_items = [d for d in expected_debits if d in bank_statement_feed]
        for item in cleared_items:
            self.add_to_permanent_ledger(item)
            
        return pending_reconciliation

    def add_to_permanent_ledger(self, transaction):
        # This is the 'Source of Truth'
        self.ledger_history.append(transaction)
```

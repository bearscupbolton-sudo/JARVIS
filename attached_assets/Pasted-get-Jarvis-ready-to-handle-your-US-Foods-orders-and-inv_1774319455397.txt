Since you’re running a tight ship at Bear’s Cup, the goal is to have Jarvis automatically distinguish between what’s ordered versus what actually arrived (the invoice).
Here is the criteria to feed into your Jarvis development:
1. The Trigger (Input Sources)
Jarvis needs to monitor your bakery’s inbox for two distinct types of emails from US Foods:
• Order Confirmations: Usually sent immediately after you place an order.
• Digital Invoices: Sent after delivery (e.g., your recent ones from March 21 and 20).
• Keywords to watch: "Order Confirmation", "Invoice #", "ACH Payment Notification", and "Will Call Invoice".
2. Header Data (The "Identity" Fields)
Jarvis must extract these to categorize the entry:
• Vendor: US Foods
• Location: (Crucial since you have Saratoga and Bolton Landing) – Look for the shipping address to tag the correct "Location ID."
• Invoice/Order Number: Unique ID for tracking.
• Date: Invoice Date vs. Delivery Date.
• Total Amount: To match against your bank/ACH records.
3. Line Item Extraction (The Granular Stuff)
This is where Jarvis adds real value. He needs to pull:
• Product Description: (e.g., "Whole Milk," "Oat Milk," "Heavy Whipping Cream").
• Pack/Size: US Foods uses specific codes like 6/10# or 12/32oz. Jarvis needs to keep these separate so you can track unit costs.
• Quantity: Number of cases or "eaches" delivered.
• Unit Price & Extended Price: To catch price creeping on your core ingredients (like flour and eggs).
4. Logic & Validation (The "Jarvis Brain")
Tell Jarvis to perform these checks:
• The "Short" Check: Flag any line items where the "Quantity Ordered" does not match "Quantity Shipped" (to catch out-of-stocks).
• Price Variance: Alert you if the price of a staple (like your King Arthur flour or high-fat butter) increases by more than 5% since the last invoice.
• Substitution Alert: Flag the "Subbed product indicator" so you know if they swapped your preferred milk or flour for a different brand.

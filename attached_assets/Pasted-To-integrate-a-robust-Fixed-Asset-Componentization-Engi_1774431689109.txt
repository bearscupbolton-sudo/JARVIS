### **2. The Journal Entry (JE) Logic**
The RAgent must handle the "moving of money" between the P&L and the Balance Sheet automatically. For a $30,000 purchase split into three $10k items, the entries look like this:

**Initial Purchase Entry (The "Lump" Credit):**
* **Debit:** 1510 - Machinery & Equipment (Fixed Asset) | $30,000.00
* **Credit:** 1010 - Cash/Operating | $30,000.00
* *Ramification:* No impact on your "Net Income" for the month. Your cash goes down, but your "Total Assets" stay the same because the value moved from Cash to Equipment.

**Monthly Depreciation Entry (The "Recurring" Debit):**
* **Debit:** 6500 - Depreciation Expense (P&L) | ~$500.00 (Total for all 3 items)
* **Credit:** 1550 - Accumulated Depreciation (Balance Sheet) | $500.00
* *Ramification:* This is where the cost hits your "Net Income." By spreading that $30k over 60 months ($500/mo), your monthly reporting reflects the actual "wear and tear" cost of the equipment against the bread it produces.

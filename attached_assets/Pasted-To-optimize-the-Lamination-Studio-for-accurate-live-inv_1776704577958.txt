To optimize the Lamination Studio for accurate live inventory and reporting, I will implement a State-Driven Inventory Architecture.

The current system has a major "leak": it treats a batch of dough as a single object that either exists or is deleted. To fix this and provide the reporting you need, we must transition to a system that tracks individual piece counts through three specific stages.

1. The "Partial-Bake" Inventory Logic
I will rewrite the handleBakeFromBox function to stop marking entire records as "baked".

The Change: When you bake 12 out of 30 croissants, Jarvis will decrement the proofPieces count by 12 and update the shapings array.

The Benefit: The remaining 18 pieces stay in the "Retarder Proofer" state, keeping your live inventory counts 100% accurate throughout the shift.

2. Implementation of a "Bake-Off Ledger"
Currently, reporting is difficult because baked items are just "status updates" on dough records. I will optimize this by routing every bake-off through the quickLogMutation logic.

The Change: Every time a "Bake Off" button is pressed in a retarder box, Jarvis will automatically create a new entry in the bakeoff-logs table.

The Reporting Advantage: This creates a permanent ledger of exactly what was baked, when, and by whom, which can be fed directly into your Admin Insights and Live Inventory screens.

3. Name Normalization for Reporting
To prevent your reports from showing "Croissant" and "croissant" as different items, I will implement a normalization layer.

The Change: I will update aggregateBoxPastries to force all pastry names to Trim() and Title Case.

The Result: This ensures that regardless of how an item was added (Freezer, Direct Add, or Rack), it aggregates into a single, clean line item for your totals and inventory reports.

4. Box Assignment Hardening
I will remove the "Legacy Proof Box" dump that currently forces all rack items into Box 1.

The Change: When moving dough from the Rack to "Proofing," the system will now require you to select Box 1 or Box 2.

The Benefit: This eliminates the "junk drawer" effect and ensures that your physical retarder boxes match your digital dashboard exactly.
